This visualization shows the MIT community grouped by geography, area, or affiliation. In terms of interactivity, the user is shown additional details per demographic bucket on mouseover, via a minimalist tooltip - this way, the interactivity does not distract from the visualization and augments the user experience by providing additional detail as needed. The visualization also allows the user to view different slices of the data by academic area or affiliation, and by toggling between population count and percentage. The visualization also allows interactive sorting of the buckets by their population data- by default they are sorted alphabetically, since the groups are not ordered. The interactive sorting allows the user to quickly rank and order the buckets visually. The visualization is currently using length to encode the demographic data - since is will become a component of Project 2, the bars may also eventually be differentiated visually by color, to connect and group relevant data across other parts of the larger visualization. Since this is an initial example of a piece of the finalized project visualization, the user is currently limited to a few counties as an example. Also, in the final project a key will be provided for the area and affiliation codes.
